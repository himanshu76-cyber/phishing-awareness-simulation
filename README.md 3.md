## рҹ“Ң Overview

> A **phishing simulation** is a controlled exercise that mimics a real social-engineering attack to measure how people react вҖ” without any real-world risk to their accounts or data.

This project, developed by **Himanshu Soni** at **Naviotech Solution Pvt. Ltd.** (2026), walks through a complete phishing attack chain in a safe sandbox: a fake **"Password Reset"** email is sent, the recipient clicks through to a **fake Google sign-in page**, enters credentials, and is then shown an **awareness summary** explaining the red flags they may have missed. Their reactions are then captured through a short **feedback survey**.

### Key Findings

#### Finding 01 вҖ” High Click-Through Rate
> **83.3%** of participants clicked the login button on the fake page вҖ” showing how easily a convincing email and landing page can drive engagement.

#### Finding 02 вҖ” Near-Misses on Real Credentials
> **41.7%** of participants admitted they *almost* entered their real credentials, underscoring how natural it feels to trust a familiar-looking login screen.

#### Finding 03 вҖ” Detection Happens Late
> **50%** of participants did not notice the page was fake until they read the warning banner вҖ” detection without prompts remains a major gap.

#### Finding 04 вҖ” The Page Looked Convincing
> **41.7%** rated the fake login page a **4 out of 5** for how convincing it appeared, confirming that visual similarity alone is often enough to deceive.

#### Finding 05 вҖ” Low Starting Awareness
> **50%** of participants rated their cybersecurity awareness as **1 out of 5** *before* the demo вҖ” suggesting this kind of training fills a real knowledge gap.
